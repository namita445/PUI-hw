About Me

I’m a senior studying Human-Computer Interaction and Computer Science @ Carnegie Mellon University, super passionate about all things UX. With a strong background in coding, my goal as designer is to help bridge the gaps between research → design → development, ensuring truly meaningful experiences for users.

My Design Philosophy

Data-driven design

I strongly believe that design is objective— it loves numbers, metrics, and solutions. Design sees problems and iterates towards goals and outcomes. Data-driven design helps us:

  • Understand needs of users. Designers aren’t users-- we should understand the needs of users through generative user research and usability testing.
  • Move beyond our reliance on best practices. Following design guidelines to the letter doesn’t always allow us develop empathy. By relying on website analytics and usability test results instead, we can discover innovative opportunities to serve users better.

Importance of Conversation

I believe that conversation is at the heart of interaction. Good design should "read the room" like a good date– that magical heart-to-heart where you just get each other on a deeper level, instead of reaching for pre-prepared note cards to fill awkward silence. Designing with conversation in mind takes weaving empathy, descriptiveness, inclusivity, and humanity in every interaction.
